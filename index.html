How ClawBox AI protects your privacy — the exact data flow

"ClawBox AI is private" is a claim worth unpacking. Here is what actually happens when you use ClawBox AI:

  1. Your message enters ClawBox AI over your local network. Telegram, WhatsApp, Discord, or the web UI all route messages through the encrypted channel you already use with that platform — and the final hop terminates on your ClawBox AI, not on a cloud server.
  2. ClawBox AI runs inference locally. The model weights, the context window, the prompt, and the response all live on the ClawBox AI NVMe drive. Nothing about that turn is sent to OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, or anyone else unless you explicitly configure an external API skill.
  3. History is stored on the ClawBox AI device only. Conversation memory, learned preferences, and any documents you upload all stay on the ClawBox AI SSD. You can wipe ClawBox AI at any time with a factory-reset button — no cloud sync, no orphaned data anywhere.
  4. Outbound traffic is explicit. ClawBox AI only phones outward when a skill needs to (fetch a website, send a Telegram message, call a weather API). There is no background telemetry from ClawBox AI itself — no crash reporting, no analytics, no usage beaconing.

That means for a journalist, a lawyer, a therapist, or a privacy-conscious family, ClawBox AI is a real answer to the "we do not want our conversations on someone else's server" requirement. Many ChatGPT users get uncomfortable the moment a prompt mentions a client, a patient, or a source — ClawBox AI removes that friction because the ClawBox AI box itself is the only place that sees the data.
